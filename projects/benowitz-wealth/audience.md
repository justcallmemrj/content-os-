# Audience — Benowitz Wealth Management

## frs-50plus (primary)

Florida Retirement System members: teachers, deputies and police, firefighters,
EMS, corrections officers, state/county employees. One pension, one DROP
account, maybe a 457, a house. A small number of very large, mostly
irreversible decisions. Suspicious of advisors — earned and correct; respect it.
Not fragile; they run toward what others run from.

**Topic bank (where the irreversible choices live):** DROP entry and exit
(election deadlines, payout mechanics, rollover-vs-check, the year the money
lands) · Pension Plan vs. Investment Plan and the 2nd Election (survivor-benefit
consequences) · Social Security (WEP/GPO repeal changed the calculus for members
with outside earnings — high-value, high-confusion) · health insurance between
retirement and 65 (the gap almost nobody plans for) · survivor Options 1–4
(chosen at retirement, permanent, frequently chosen in five minutes) · 457(b)
and 403(b) — what they're for and not for · the last three years of service
(the AFC calculation makes them matter).

## linkedin-referrers (secondary)

FRS members are not on LinkedIn looking for retirement help. The people there
stand next to the client: district benefits coordinators, union reps, sheriff's
office HR, CPAs. Write to the professional beside the client; the CTA is
*forward this*, never *book a call*. LinkedIn is a referral channel wearing a
content channel's clothes.

## What to avoid

Fear-mongering about pension solvency (live political topic; using it to sell
is exactly what made this audience suspicious). Any implication FRS is doing
something wrong or hiding something — FRS administers a pension; it doesn't do
financial planning, and saying so plainly requires no villain. Any suggestion
Benowitz can get a better pension outcome — the pension is the pension; what's
decidable is everything around it.
