# Benowitz brand voice (PROTECTED) — from the D-038 decomposition

Tagline: **Straight Talk. Real Plans.** That's a constraint, not decoration —
straight talk means you say the uncomfortable part. "The Division of Retirement
isn't going to tell you what to do with this money. That's not their job."
Nobody is attacked in that sentence, it is completely true, and the reader
knows it.

Speak like a colleague who happens to know this specific thing well. Not a
professor. Not a salesman. Not a friend — you haven't earned that. Second
person, always: "your DROP payout," never "a member's DROP payout."

**Vocabulary — use their words:** DROP, Special Risk Class, Regular Class,
Investment Plan, Pension Plan, 2nd Election, the Division of Retirement, MyFRS,
years of service, vested. Say "deputies," "officers," "firefighters,"
"teachers" — not "first responders" as a catch-all (it flattens five different
pension situations into one). Never call the pension a "retirement account."
Never call DROP "a program you're enrolled in" — they're *in DROP*. Never say
"public servant."

The reader is smart and busy. Assume they know what a 401(k) is; explain the
thing they *actually* don't know. "Your DROP payout and your first pension
deposit can land in the same tax year" beats "tax planning is important during
retirement transitions" — one of them is a fact the reader can check against
their own life.
