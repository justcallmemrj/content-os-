▶ FACEBOOK CAPTION
A detail that catches a lot of FRS members off guard: the cost-of-living adjustment. If you were initially enrolled on or after July 1, 2011, your Pension Plan benefit does not receive a COLA after you retire — it stays flat. For service earned before July 2011, a partial COLA applies (calculated by a formula). Why it matters: a fixed pension with no inflation adjustment slowly loses purchasing power over a long retirement — a bigger deal for those who retire young, like many in Special Risk. It’s a strong reason to build some inflation protection into the rest of your plan.

▶ LINKEDIN CAPTION
A frequently overlooked feature of the FRS Pension Plan: the post-retirement cost-of-living adjustment depends heavily on your enrollment date. Members initially enrolled on or after July 1, 2011 receive no COLA on their pension benefit after retirement. For service earned before that date, a partial COLA applies, calculated as (years of service before July 2011 ÷ total years of service) × 3%. A member with half their service before July 2011, for instance, would receive a 1.5% adjustment rather than 3%. The planning implication is meaningful: a fixed pension without inflation adjustment gradually loses purchasing power over a multi-decade retirement — a particularly relevant concern for those who retire young, including many Special Risk members.
