▶ FACEBOOK CAPTION
Important DROP update that not everyone has caught: the rules changed in 2023. Under SB 7024, effective July 1, 2023, the maximum DROP participation period increased from 5 years to 8 years (and to 10 years for certain K-12 instructional personnel). The interest rate on your accumulating DROP balance rose from 1.3% to 4%. And the old requirement to enter within 12 months of reaching normal retirement was eliminated. If your understanding of DROP is based on the older rules, the math may look quite different now. Always confirm the current specifics with MyFRS.

▶ LINKEDIN CAPTION
A significant update that some FRS members haven’t fully registered: the Deferred Retirement Option Program changed materially in 2023. Under SB 7024, effective July 1, 2023, the maximum DROP participation period increased from 5 years to 8 years — and to 10 years for certain K-12 instructional personnel. The interest rate on the accumulating DROP balance increased from 1.3% to 4%. And the restrictive 12-month entry window was removed — members may now enter DROP at any time after reaching normal retirement. Both the longer participation period and the higher interest rate can change the calculus on whether and when DROP makes sense. For anyone whose understanding predates these changes, it’s worth revisiting — and confirming the current rules and your own figures directly with MyFRS.
