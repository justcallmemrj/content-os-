▶ LINKEDIN CAPTION
Among the more avoidable and consequential retirement missteps is mistiming Medicare enrollment. Your Initial Enrollment Period (IEP) is a seven-month window: the three months before the month you turn 65, your birthday month, and the three months after. Missing it — without qualifying employer coverage that would grant a Special Enrollment Period — can result in permanent late-enrollment penalties (the Part B penalty adds 10% to your premium for each 12-month period you delayed, for life) and a gap in coverage until the next opportunity. The remedy is simple: review your Medicare timing before you turn 65, with particular attention to how Medicare coordinates with any employer or retiree coverage you hold. A note on our role: we are a fee-only fiduciary, not an insurance agency. We don’t sell or enroll anyone in Medicare plans — our purpose is to help you understand the deadlines and how the pieces fit.
