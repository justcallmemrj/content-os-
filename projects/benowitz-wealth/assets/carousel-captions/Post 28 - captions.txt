▶ FACEBOOK CAPTION
A DROP rule change worth knowing: the restrictive entry window is gone. It used to be that you generally had to enter DROP within 12 months of reaching your normal retirement date, or forfeit the opportunity. As of July 1, 2023 (under SB 7024), that 12-month window was eliminated — you can now enter DROP at any time after you’ve reached normal retirement and become vested. This gives you more flexibility in planning your retirement timing. That said, a couple of things still hold: your pension benefit locks in when you enter DROP, and your participation period is finite. So the question of when to enter remains an important one. Confirm the current rules and your own dates with MyFRS.

▶ LINKEDIN CAPTION
Among the 2023 changes to the Deferred Retirement Option Program, one meaningfully expands flexibility: the elimination of the restrictive entry window. Previously, members generally had to elect DROP within 12 months of reaching their normal retirement date or forfeit the opportunity. Under SB 7024, effective July 1, 2023, that window was removed. Members may now enter DROP at any time after reaching normal retirement and vesting — not solely within the first year. This adds genuine flexibility to retirement timing. Two considerations remain, however: the pension benefit locks in at the moment of DROP entry, and the participation period is finite (now up to 8 years for most classes, 10 for certain K-12 instructional personnel). So while the opportunity to enter is no longer time-boxed, the decision of when to enter still carries real weight and merits planning. Confirm current rules and your specific dates with MyFRS.
