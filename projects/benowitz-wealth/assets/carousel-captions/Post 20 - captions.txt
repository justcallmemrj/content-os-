▶ FACEBOOK CAPTION
One of the three numbers behind your FRS pension is your Average Final Compensation (AFC) — and which years count depends on when you enrolled. If you joined before July 1, 2011 (Tier 1), your AFC is the average of your highest 5 fiscal years of salary. If you joined on or after (Tier 2), it’s your highest 8. AFC can also include up to 500 hours of lump-sum annual leave payout, within agency limits. Because AFC multiplies against your years of service and your class percentage, your highest-earning years carry real weight — which is why planning your final working years deliberately can matter. ▶ LINKEDIN CAPTION
Average Final Compensation (AFC) is one of the three inputs to the FRS pension formula — and which salary years count depends on your enrollment tier. Tier 1 (enrolled before July 1, 2011): AFC is the average of your highest 5 fiscal years of salary. Tier 2 (on or after July 1, 2011): the average of your highest 8. AFC may also include up to 500 hours of lump-sum annual leave payout, subject to agency limits. Because AFC multiplies against your years of service and class percentage, your highest-earning years carry meaningful weight in the final benefit. For members approaching retirement, this is one reason the timing of the final working years — and how salary, promotions, and leave payouts fall — can influence the outcome.
