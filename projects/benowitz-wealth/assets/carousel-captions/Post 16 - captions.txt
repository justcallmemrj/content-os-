▶ FACEBOOK CAPTION
Here’s an FRS feature many members forget they have: the 2nd Election. During your career, you get one opportunity to switch between the Pension Plan and the Investment Plan. A key detail: if you switch from the Investment Plan to the Pension Plan, you have to “buy in” — paying the present value (the actuarial cost) of the pension benefit, usually from your account balance and sometimes from personal funds. The good news is that service in one plan generally counts toward vesting in the other. Because this is a one-time and often-irreversible decision with real cost implications, it’s worth thinking through carefully — ideally by modeling it first. Confirm the specifics with MyFRS.

▶ LINKEDIN CAPTION
A feature of the FRS that members sometimes overlook — and occasionally treat too casually — is the 2nd Election. Most members receive one opportunity during their career to switch between the Pension Plan and the Investment Plan. The mechanics matter: moving from the Investment Plan to the Pension Plan requires a “buy-in” — paying the present value (actuarial cost) of the accrued pension benefit, typically from the Investment Plan balance and, if needed, personal funds. Service in either plan generally counts toward the other’s vesting requirement. Because this is a one-time, frequently irreversible decision with significant financial implications, it warrants careful analysis rather than a snap judgment. Modeling the outcome — and confirming the buy-in cost with MyFRS — is the prudent approach before exercising it.
