▶ FACEBOOK CAPTION
So many of the most important retirement choices — when to enter DROP, which pension payout option to elect, when to claim Social Security — are far easier when you approach them early, with time to think. Decisions made under deadline pressure are rarely your best ones. The earlier you understand your options, the more options you actually have.

▶ LINKEDIN CAPTION
A principle that holds across nearly every retirement decision: the best ones are made early and calmly, not in the compressed weeks before a deadline. DROP entry, pension payout elections, Social Security timing, Medicare enrollment — each is more sound when approached with time to understand the trade-offs and model the outcomes. Deadline pressure narrows options and elevates the risk of an avoidable mistake. The earlier the planning, the wider the range of choices that remain genuinely available.
