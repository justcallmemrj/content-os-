▶ FACEBOOK CAPTION
Ever compare FRS notes with a coworker and find your rules don’t match? There’s usually one explanation: your enrollment dates fall on different sides of July 1, 2011. That single date is the dividing line for several things at once — vesting (6 years if you enrolled before, 8 if after), Average Final Compensation (highest 5 years vs highest 8), normal retirement age, and the cost-of-living adjustment (service before July 2011 earns a partial COLA; those who enrolled after get none). So when you hear conflicting “facts” about FRS, the enrollment date is often the reason. Knowing which side of the line you’re on tells you which rules actually apply to you. Confirm your status with MyFRS.

▶ LINKEDIN CAPTION
A single date — July 1, 2011 — quietly governs how multiple FRS rules apply to a given member, and it’s frequently the reason two members encounter seemingly contradictory “facts.” Whether you were initially enrolled before or on/after July 1, 2011 affects, simultaneously: vesting (6 years vs. 8 years); Average Final Compensation (highest 5 fiscal years vs. highest 8); normal retirement age (different thresholds by tier); and COLA (service before July 2011 earns a partial cost-of-living adjustment; members enrolled on/after receive none). The practical implication: general FRS “rules of thumb” can be misleading unless anchored to enrollment date. Knowing which side of the line you fall on determines how every other provision applies to you — and it’s the first thing to confirm with MyFRS when planning.
