DROP might be the most misunderstood FRS benefit — so here it is in plain English. You reach normal retirement, “retire” on paper, but keep working. ▶ FACEBOOK CAPTION
Let’s demystify DROP. The Deferred Retirement Option Program lets eligible FRS Pension Plan members reach normal retirement, officially “retire,” and keep working — while the monthly pension they’d be collecting accumulates in a trust fund earning interest. When they truly leave, that accumulated balance is paid out or rolled over. It can be a powerful tool. It’s also Pension-Plan-only, and the decision of when to enter is one you want to think through carefully.

▶ LINKEDIN CAPTION
The Deferred Retirement Option Program (DROP) is among the most valuable features of the FRS Pension Plan — and among the most misunderstood. Once you reach normal retirement, you may elect DROP: you “retire” for benefit-calculation purposes — your monthly pension amount is locked in — but you continue working. Instead of being paid to you, that monthly benefit accumulates in a trust fund earning interest. When you ultimately separate, you receive the accumulated balance as a lump sum or roll it into a tax-deferred account, and your lifetime monthly pension begins. Two things matter most: DROP is available only to Pension Plan members, and because your benefit locks in at entry, when you enter is a consequential decision.
