▶ FACEBOOK CAPTION
If you’re in the FRS, you chose between the Pension Plan and the Investment Plan — maybe years ago, maybe without anyone explaining the difference. In plain terms: the Pension Plan pays a guaranteed monthly benefit for life, calculated by a formula. The Investment Plan is a 401(k)-style account you own and direct, where the State doesn’t guarantee the outcome — you do. Both take 3% of your salary. The real question is whether you’d rather have certainty or control. We help Florida public employees think it through without the jargon.

▶ LINKEDIN CAPTION
One of the most consequential financial decisions a Florida public employee makes happens early — often on day one — and frequently without a clear explanation: Pension Plan or Investment Plan? The Pension Plan is a defined-benefit plan: a guaranteed monthly benefit for life, determined by a formula. The Investment Plan is a defined-contribution plan: an individual account you own and direct, similar to a 401(k), where the eventual benefit depends on contributions, market performance, and fees. Both require the same 3% employee contribution. What differs is who bears the investment risk — the State, or you. Neither plan is universally superior; the right fit depends on expected career length, other assets, and risk tolerance.
