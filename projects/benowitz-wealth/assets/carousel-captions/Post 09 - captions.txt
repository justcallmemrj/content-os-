▶ FACEBOOK CAPTION
A point that matters more than people realize: Special Risk classification is based on your job duties, not your title. The class covers Florida public employees whose primary work is protecting the public — law enforcement officers, firefighters, correctional officers, and certain EMS personnel. Because those careers are physically demanding and often shorter, Special Risk members earn a higher pension accrual rate: 3% per year of service, compared with 1.6% for Regular Class. That difference adds up significantly over a career. If your role has changed over time, it’s worth confirming how each period of your service is classified.

▶ LINKEDIN CAPTION
Special Risk Class membership in the FRS is determined by the duties of the position — not the job title. The class covers Florida public employees whose primary responsibilities involve protection of the public: law enforcement officers, firefighters, correctional officers, probation officers, and certain emergency medical personnel. Because these careers are physically demanding and tend to be shorter, Special Risk members accrue pension benefits at a higher rate — 3% per year of creditable service, versus 1.6% for Regular Class. Over a full career, that difference is substantial. Because classification is duty-based, an employee whose responsibilities change may move between Regular and Special Risk — and only service actually worked in a Special Risk position accrues at 3%. Confirm with MyFRS exactly how each period of your service is classified.
