▶ FACEBOOK CAPTION
A question we hear a lot: do FRS members receive Social Security on top of their pension? In most cases, yes. Unlike public employees in certain other states whose jobs don’t participate in Social Security, most FRS-covered employment in Florida is Social Security–covered — you pay in through payroll taxes and earn benefits accordingly. (This is also why the recent repeal of WEP and GPO generally didn’t change things for a typical FRS-only career.) Your specific situation can still vary depending on your employer and work history, so it’s worth confirming. For many members, retirement income comes from two main sources — the pension and Social Security — and the real value is in coordinating them.

▶ LINKEDIN CAPTION
A common source of confusion: do FRS members receive Social Security in addition to their pension? In most cases, yes. Public employees in some states work in positions not covered by Social Security — but most FRS-covered employment in Florida is Social Security–covered, meaning members pay in through payroll taxes and earn benefits alongside their FRS pension. This is also the key to understanding why the recent repeal of WEP and GPO generally didn’t alter outcomes for a typical FRS-only career: those provisions applied specifically to benefits connected with non-covered pensions. Individual situations can still vary by employer and work history, so verifying your own record with the Social Security Administration is worthwhile. For many members, retirement income rests on two pillars — the pension and Social Security — and the planning value lies in coordinating them.
