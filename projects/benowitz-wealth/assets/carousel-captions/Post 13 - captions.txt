▶ FACEBOOK CAPTION
Here’s the formula behind your FRS pension, in plain terms: Average Final Compensation × Years of Service × Class Percentage = your annual benefit. Your AFC is the average of your highest 5 years of salary if you enrolled before July 2011, or your highest 8 years if you enrolled after. The class percentage is 1.6% per year for Regular Class and 3% for Special Risk. So a Regular Class member with 30 years of service has accrued 48% of their AFC (30 × 1.6%). That’s a simplified illustration — your actual numbers depend on your record — but understanding the moving parts is the first step to planning around them.

▶ LINKEDIN CAPTION
The FRS Pension Plan benefit isn’t arbitrary — it’s a formula, and understanding it is the foundation of planning around it. Average Final Compensation (AFC) × Years of Creditable Service × Membership Class Percentage = annual benefit. AFC is the average of your highest 5 fiscal years of salary if you enrolled before July 1, 2011, or your highest 8 fiscal years if you enrolled on or after. Class percentage is 1.6% per year for Regular Class; 3% for Special Risk. As an illustration, a Regular Class member with 30 years has accrued 48% of AFC (30 × 1.6%); a Special Risk member with 25 years has accrued 75% (25 × 3%). These are simplified examples — actual benefits depend on your record and elections. Because the inputs multiply, each one matters: confirming your figures with MyFRS, and planning your final working years deliberately, can be valuable.
