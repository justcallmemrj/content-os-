▶ FACEBOOK CAPTION
One of the biggest Social Security changes in years: the Social Security Fairness Act, signed January 5, 2025, repealed two long-standing provisions — the Windfall Elimination Provision (WEP) and the Government Pension Offset (GPO) — retroactive to January 2024. Here’s the part that matters for accuracy: WEP and GPO only ever reduced benefits connected to a pension from work that was NOT covered by Social Security. Most FRS employment in Florida is Social Security–covered, so many FRS members were never subject to these rules in the first place. Where the repeal really matters is if you — or a spouse — ever had a pension from non-covered work: prior government service elsewhere, certain local plans, or federal CSRS. If that describes your situation, it’s worth reviewing your record with the SSA.

▶ LINKEDIN CAPTION
The Social Security Fairness Act, signed into law on January 5, 2025, represents one of the most significant changes to Social Security for public-sector workers in decades. It repealed the Windfall Elimination Provision (WEP) and the Government Pension Offset (GPO), with relief retroactive to January 2024. An important nuance often lost in the headlines: WEP and GPO only ever affected benefits connected to a pension earned in employment not covered by Social Security. Most FRS-covered employment in Florida participates in Social Security — meaning a member with a “pure” FRS career generally was not subject to these provisions to begin with. Where the repeal is most relevant is for individuals (or their spouses) who had a pension from non-covered work: prior government service in a non-covered system in another state, certain municipal plans outside the FRS, or federal CSRS. If your history includes non-covered government employment, it’s worth verifying your record directly with the Social Security Administration.
