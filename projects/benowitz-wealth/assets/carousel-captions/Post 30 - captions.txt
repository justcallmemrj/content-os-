▶ FACEBOOK CAPTION
If you own your home in Florida, make sure you’re taking the homestead exemption — it’s an easy way to lower your property taxes that some homeowners miss. The exemption can reduce your primary residence’s assessed value by up to $50,000 for property-tax purposes. To qualify, you must own and occupy the home as your permanent residence as of January 1, and you generally need to apply by March 1. As a bonus, claiming homestead also unlocks the Save Our Homes assessment cap, which limits how much your assessed value can rise each year. It isn’t automatic for everyone — new homeowners in particular sometimes forget to file. If you’ve recently bought or moved, confirm with your county property appraiser that you’re set up. (Note: this property-tax exemption is separate from Florida’s homestead creditor protection — a different benefit.)

▶ LINKEDIN CAPTION
A practical Florida property-tax benefit that homeowners — especially recent buyers — sometimes overlook: the homestead exemption. The exemption can reduce a primary residence’s assessed value by up to $50,000 for property-tax purposes. Eligibility requires owning and occupying the home as your permanent residence as of January 1, with an application generally due by March 1. Claiming homestead also activates the Save Our Homes assessment limitation, which caps annual increases in assessed value. Two clarifications worth noting. First, it isn’t automatic for everyone — those who’ve recently purchased or relocated should confirm with their county property appraiser that they’ve filed. Second, this property-tax exemption is distinct from Florida’s constitutional homestead creditor protection; they’re separate benefits that share a name. Property-tax mechanics vary by county, so confirm specifics locally.
