▶ FACEBOOK CAPTION
Quick FRS term that’s worth getting right: vesting. It’s the amount of service you need before you truly own your benefit. For the Pension Plan, that’s 6 years if you enrolled before July 1, 2011, or 8 years if you enrolled on or after. For the Investment Plan, it’s just 1 year — and your own contributions belong to you immediately. Why it matters: leaving before you’re vested in the Pension Plan can mean leaving the benefit behind. If you’re close, know where you stand.

▶ LINKEDIN CAPTION
“Vesting” is one of those FRS terms that’s easy to nod along to and costly to misunderstand. It’s the service threshold at which you own your benefit. The two plans differ significantly: the Pension Plan vests at 6 years of creditable service if you enrolled before July 1, 2011, or 8 years if you enrolled on or after that date; the Investment Plan vests at 1 year of service, and your own employee contributions are vested immediately. The practical implication: separating from FRS-covered employment before you’re vested in the Pension Plan can mean forfeiting the benefit. For anyone near a vesting milestone who is contemplating a career move, the timing is worth confirming precisely — with MyFRS — before acting.
