▶ FACEBOOK CAPTION
If Medicare feels like alphabet soup, here’s the simple version. Part A is hospital coverage — most people pay no premium. Part B is doctors and outpatient care, with a monthly premium. Part C (Medicare Advantage) is a private all-in-one alternative to Original Medicare. Part D is prescription drugs. Original Medicare is A + B, and many people add D and/or a supplement. Understanding these four pieces makes the rest far less overwhelming. Note: we’re a fiduciary firm, not an insurance agency — our role here is purely to explain.

▶ LINKEDIN CAPTION
Medicare’s terminology is a barrier for a lot of people approaching 65. Reduced to essentials, it’s four parts: Part A — inpatient/hospital coverage, with most beneficiaries paying no premium. Part B — outpatient and physician services, carrying a monthly premium. Part C — Medicare Advantage: private, all-in-one plans that bundle coverage as an alternative to Original Medicare. Part D — prescription drug coverage through private plans. Original Medicare is Parts A and B; many add Part D and/or a supplement.
