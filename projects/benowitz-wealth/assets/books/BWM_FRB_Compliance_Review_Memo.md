## §1 · Regulatory posture

The series is educational marketing by a state-registered investment adviser and should be treated as an **advertisement** under the SEC Marketing Rule framework (206(4)-1, as applied to state registrants via Florida rule alignment) and Florida Ch. 517 / DFS-OFR standards. Drafting posture throughout is deliberately conservative:

1. **Education-only, no individualized advice.** Every chapter routes decisions to agencies (MyFRS/DR, SSA, Medicare/SHINE) and engaged professionals (tax, legal, insurance, adviser). No "you should" directives on any elective decision; hedged constructions ("may," "depending on your situation," "a framework for questions") used throughout. Verified by drafting QA scan (banned-phrase list, all three manuscripts + launch kit: zero hits).
2. **No performance advertising.** No returns cited as achievable, no projections, no charts of hypothetical figures (illustrations are static, in-text, labeled). The only rate references are program facts (DROP 4% statutory interest; Social Security delayed credits) and research descriptions (the 4% withdrawal literature, explicitly framed as "a research finding about the past... not a law, not a guarantee").
3. **No testimonials/endorsements.** All persons fictional, chip-labeled at every scene; disclosure blocks state "do not describe actual clients or actual results."
4. **Hypotheticals policy.** Every numeric walk-through opens with a "Hypothetical" label; master blocks reinforce. Figures chosen to be pedagogically plausible, not aspirational.
5. **Fee-only claim (ACTION ITEM — highest priority).** Benowitz materials state **fee-only** (site-verbatim). Ducat materials for the same RIA state **fee-based**. Before print: confirm Form ADV Part 2A (and any Part 2A Appendix/CRS) describes compensation such that both brand-level statements are accurate — e.g., ADV discloses advisory-fee-only compensation firm-wide (making "fee-based" at Ducat merely imprecise-but-permissible, though it should then be conformed), or discloses distinct programs. If firm-wide compensation is strictly advisory fees, recommend conforming Ducat's language to "fee-only" at next refresh; if any non-advisory compensation exists anywhere, the Benowitz "fee-only" claim must be corrected instead. Document the determination in the file.
6. **Registration language.** CRD #331545 + adviserinfo.sec.gov + "registration does not imply skill/approval" + jurisdiction limitation appear in every master block and the standard footer. Book 3 additionally carries the Ch. 12 bolded conflict box (below).

## §2 · Non-affiliation controls (elevated risk area)

Audience is government-adjacent; implied-endorsement risk is the series' largest exposure. Controls in place:
- Bolded non-affiliation sentence in every master block (copyright page + back matter, all books), every back cover, every worksheet footer, the standard landing/email footer, and the social micro-footer. Wording matches the site's notice and enumerates FRS, State, counties, municipalities, districts, sheriff/police/fire, public employers, SSA, Medicare.
- Design rule (binding on Claude Design/production): no FRS, state, agency, or association logos, seals, badges, uniforms, or agency-look trade dress anywhere in the series or its promotion; no school/agency colors as identity cues.
- Editorial rule: the books describe programs; they never speak *for* them ("confirm with MyFRS" framing throughout; "current as of July 2026" date-stamps on rule statements).
- Cross-brand separation: Benowitz and Ducat materials never co-appear; funnels, footers, and disclosures are brand-specific.

## §4 · Content-specific rulings (drafting decisions of record)

1. **Pension options (B2 Ch. 2–3):** all four presented neutrally; no ranking, no default recommendation; the insurance-alternative ("pension maximization") section examines rather than endorses/condemns, and flags commission incentives symmetrically. Spousal acknowledgment described as awareness-only — accurate to the form's legal effect.
2. **DROP (B1 Ch. 6–7; B2 Ch. 4):** timing framed as fact-dependent with opposite correct answers illustrated (Washington/Green); entry/exit rules hedged with "confirm current rules/deadlines in writing"; option-election-at-entry stated (accuracy-critical). 2029 instructional sunset flagged twice with verify-before-relying language.
3. **Social Security/Medicare (B2 Ch. 7–9; B3 Ch. 5):** education only; no claiming recommendations (framings — pension-floor, survivor consolidation — are analytical lenses, not directives); WEP/GPO repeal stated with effective dates; all 2026 figures date-stamped; SHINE named as the sales-free resource; firm's non-enrollment/non-insurance status stated.
4. **Tax content (B2 Ch. 10; B3 Ch. 5):** concepts + professional referral; Roth conversions framed as "a conversation," never a directive, always with IRMAA/marketplace interaction warnings; OBBBA senior deduction flagged temporary (2025–2028) with phase-out caveat; QCD limit stated as "roughly $110,000, indexed."
5. **Products/annuities (B3 Ch. 6):** category treated as legitimate tool; criticism aimed at sales process/architecture, never at named products, carriers, or persons; Ruth arc deliberately includes an appropriate purchase to preserve balance. Fraud section names tactic patterns only.
6. **New Special Risk COLA (all books):** described with conditions (5-years-retired wait, service requirements) and "verify enactment/current status" hedges — **refresh item #1 below.**
7. **Reemployment (B3 Ch. 11):** rules summarized at awareness level only, with mandatory "call the Division, get it in writing, before any FRS-employer position" directive — the one place a hard directive is used, and it directs to the agency, not the firm.
8. **LTC figures (B3 Ch. 7):** stated as "roughly/commonly" ranges attributed to industry surveys; Medicaid planning routed exclusively to elder-law attorneys.
