# RIA compliance envelope — PROTECTED, shared by explicit include only (Phase 3 §3.5)

Joy Financial Group LLC is a Florida state-registered investment adviser. Both
consumer brands (Benowitz Wealth Management, Ducat Private Wealth) operate under
it. Every public post is an **advertisement** under the SEC Marketing Rule and
is subject to Florida OFR oversight. This envelope shapes what copy can say; it
is not a formality bolted on afterward.

## The four hard lines

**1. No performance claims, no projections, no guarantees.** Anything of the
form "this could save you $X," "grow your money by," "maximize your payout," or
"get more from your pension" is a performance representation. So is "most
people leave money on the table" — it implies a quantified outcome the firm can
deliver. Rewrite as a *consideration*, not a *result*:
- ✗ "Rolling over your DROP could save you thousands in taxes."
- ✓ "Whether you roll it over or take the check changes how it's taxed. That's worth understanding before the deadline."

**2. No testimonials or endorsements.** Not a client quote. Not "a client told
me." Not an anonymized success story. Not a screenshot. Not "join hundreds of
members who…" Testimonials require disclosures the format cannot carry, so the
answer in social is simply no. Composite or hypothetical scenarios are also
risky; if one is genuinely necessary it must be labeled hypothetical and must
not imply a typical result.

**3. Education, not individualized advice.** The reader is a stranger. The frame
is always how a thing *works* or what to *ask*, never what the reader *should do*.
- ✗ "Take the lump sum and roll it into an IRA."
- ✓ "Ask what a direct rollover does to your tax year, and what it costs you in access."

**4. Non-affiliation, stated on every piece.** Silence is an implied claim.
Each brand's `disclosures.md` carries the verbatim texts.

## Standing rules (firm-wide)

- **"Fee-only" is on hold** pending Form ADV wording verification across both
  brands (decision record: `projects/benowitz-wealth/decisions/DEC-BEN-0001.md`).
  Use "fiduciary." If asked explicitly for "fee-only," substitute and flag the
  substitution in the intervention note.
- **No real named public figures** as examples, positive or negative.
- **Tax content requires a referral line** to a qualified tax professional; the
  firm does not provide tax preparation.
- **Comparative claims about other advisors** invite trouble. Describe what the
  firm does; let the contrast do its own work.
- **When unsure: write the softer version and flag it.** A post that underclaims
  costs a little engagement. A post that overclaims costs an examination.
