The Foundations track, in one checklist. How many are actually in place?

□ Business and personal money in separate accounts
□ A share of every payment set aside for taxes
□ Quarterly tax dates calendared, a tax pro engaged
□ An emergency fund, funded
□ Roth IRA opened, if there's earned income
□ Credit history started clean
□ Every advisor's record checked — BrokerCheck / IAPD
□ Fiduciary status asked, answered in writing
□ Contracts read for term, exclusivity, likeness — attorney on call
□ Family money policy set: splits, asks, who says no
□ Insurance evaluated for where the career actually is
□ A monthly money review on the calendar

Twelve boxes. None optional. All buildable in a season.

Twelve weeks of foundations condense to twelve checkboxes. For an athlete family, this is the audit worth running each season:

Structure — Business income separated from personal. A fixed share of every payment routed to a tax account on arrival. Quarterly estimated-tax dates on the calendar, with a professional sizing them.

Security — An emergency fund covering several months, fully funded before serious investing. Insurance — disability, and loss-of-value where warranted — evaluated against realistic projections.

Growth — A Roth IRA opened and funded within current IRS limits, wherever there's earned income. Credit history started early and kept clean. Compounding given its head start.

Protection — Every professional's record verified through BrokerCheck and IAPD. Fiduciary status confirmed in writing. Contracts read for deliverables, term, exclusivity, and likeness rights, with an attorney reviewing anything significant.

Governance — A family money policy: the split every check follows, the answer when people ask, and who delivers it. A monthly review, twenty minutes, standing.

Nothing on the list requires wealth to start — only decisions. The families who check these boxes early tend to be the ones for whom the boxes never become emergencies.
