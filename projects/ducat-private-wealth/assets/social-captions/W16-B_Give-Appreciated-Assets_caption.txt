Here's a piece of giving mechanics most people learn years too late: how you give can matter almost as much as how much.

Say an investment you've held has grown. Sell it to donate the cash, and the sale itself can trigger capital-gains tax — the charity gets what's left after the IRS.

Donate the investment itself — directly, or through a donor-advised fund — and the picture generally improves twice: the gain you never realized isn't taxed to you, and the full value can go to work for the cause. You may still claim a deduction, subject to the rules.

Holding periods, limits, and paperwork all matter, so run it with a tax advisor first. Same generosity. Better math.

Do you know what your after-tax number really is? Save this for tax season.

Charitable intent usually arrives as a simple instinct: write the check. For anyone holding investments that have grown, that instinct quietly leaves money on the table — the charity's table.

The mechanism: selling an appreciated asset realizes the gain, and the gain is generally taxable. Donate cash from the proceeds and the charity receives the after-tax remainder. Donating the appreciated asset itself — long-held shares, most commonly, often via a donor-advised fund — changes the sequence: the unrealized gain is generally never taxed to you, the charity (tax-exempt) can realize the full value, and you may still be eligible to deduct the asset's fair market value, within the limits the rules set.

Two gifts of "the same amount" can therefore deliver meaningfully different value — same generosity, different arithmetic, with the difference funded by tax that never had to be paid.

The qualifiers are real: holding periods matter, deduction limits differ by asset type and income, valuation and paperwork requirements apply, and state details vary. This is precisely the kind of move to model with a qualified tax advisor before executing — ideally as part of a giving plan rather than a December scramble.

For athlete families building a giving tradition on top of early, concentrated income, this is the mechanic that makes the tradition efficient.
