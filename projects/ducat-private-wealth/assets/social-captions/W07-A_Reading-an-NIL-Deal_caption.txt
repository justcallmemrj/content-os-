Before you sign any NIL deal, find these six things in the document — they're always in there somewhere:

Deliverables — exactly what you owe: posts, appearances, hours.

Term — how long it runs, and whether it renews on its own.

Exclusivity — who you can't work with while it runs.

Payment — how much, when, and what triggers it.

Usage rights — where your name and image can appear, and for how long.

Termination — how either side gets out.

If you can't find one, that's a question. If you don't like one, that's a negotiation. Have an attorney read anything significant before you sign — always.

Every NIL contract, however friendly the brand, answers six questions. Reading a deal means finding those answers before signing.

What do I owe? (Deliverables: the posts, appearances, and time commitments — specific and countable.)
How long am I committed? (Term — including auto-renewal language that quietly extends it.)
What am I giving up elsewhere? (Exclusivity: which categories or competitors are now off-limits.)
What am I paid, and when? (Amount, schedule, and what conditions trigger payment.)
Where does my identity go? (Usage rights: the platforms, formats, and duration for which they may use your name and image — including after the deal ends.)
How does this end? (Termination rights, for both sides.)

A deal isn't good because the number is big. It's good because the obligations are ones you can meet, the restrictions are ones you can live with, and the rights you're granting are ones you understand.

For anything significant, a qualified attorney should read it before you sign. That's not distrust — it's how professionals treat their own name.
