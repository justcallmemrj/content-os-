## 1. Purpose and regulatory posture

This memo inventories the compliance features of the three-book educational series and its launch materials so the CCO can (a) verify every required disclosure is present and correctly placed, (b) evaluate the materials under applicable marketing rules, and (c) establish the ongoing review/refresh cycle.

**Framework applied conservatively.** As a Florida state-registered adviser, the firm is governed by Chapter 517, Florida Statutes, and Rule 69W administrative rules, which incorporate anti-fraud standards for adviser communications; the series was nonetheless drafted to satisfy the substantive standards of SEC Marketing Rule 206(4)-1 as a conservative benchmark, on the theory that (i) the books promote the firm's brand and may be deemed advertisements notwithstanding their educational character, and (ii) meeting the federal standard comfortably exceeds the state standard. Key drafting consequences:

- **No untrue statements or unsubstantiated claims.** All factual claims are date-stamped "as of July 2026," hedged where rules are unsettled, and sourced (each book's Sources section; series reference file to maintain URLs/access dates).
- **No testimonials or endorsements.** Zero client stories, athlete endorsements, or third-party statements appear anywhere in the series or launch kits. All narrative examples are fictional and labeled (see §4).
- **No performance advertising.** The firm advertises no performance, actual or hypothetical. Book 3 contains generic arithmetic illustrations of compounding/fee concepts (see §5) that are educational mathematics, not presentations of any strategy's performance; they are nonetheless wrapped in assumption disclosures as if the Marketing Rule's hypothetical-performance conditions applied.
- **Fair and balanced treatment of benefits/risks.** Every investing discussion pairs potential benefit with loss-of-principal risk; the standing risk sentence appears as a contextual strip on each investing chapter (see §3, Layer 2).
- **Prohibited-language screen.** Automated scans of all three manuscripts confirm zero instances of promissory phrasing ("guaranteed returns," "will grow," "you will build wealth," "risk-free") and zero of the banned sports-cliché list; two draft instances of "highlight reel" were caught and removed (Book 2 Ch. 12; Book 3 Ch. 6).

**Additional regimes touched by the content (educational discussion only; no services offered):** FTC Endorsement Guides (Book 2 Ch. 3 educates athletes on their own disclosure duties); state athlete-agent statutes and FHSAA Bylaw 9.9 (described, not practiced); tax rules (described at concept level with mandatory referrals to qualified tax professionals). The firm nowhere offers legal, tax, insurance, agency, or contract services, and each book's master disclosure states so expressly.

## 3. Disclosure architecture — the four layers (as designed and implemented)

**Layer 1 — Master block (copyright page, every book).** Elements present in all three: education-only/no individualized advice (enumerating financial, investment, tax, legal, accounting, estate, insurance, recruiting, contract, athletic-representation); services-not-provided list; consult-professionals directive; investment risk block (risk of loss / past performance / no guarantee); hypothetical-examples statement; rules-change + "current as of July 2026" + confirm-with-compliance-office; **non-affiliation** (universities, athletic departments, collectives, professional organizations, leagues, entertainment companies, agencies, government) ; registration paragraph. Book 3's copyright page adds an investment-examples paragraph and a pointer to the Ch. 11 conflict disclosure.

**Layer 2 — Contextual chapter strips (italic line under chapter title).** Implemented at:

- *Tax:* B1 Ch. 7, Ch. 8; B2 Ch. 10; B3 Ch. 7 — "general tax concepts for education only… work with a qualified tax professional."
- *Contract/legal:* B1 Ch. 3; B2 Ch. 4, 5, 6 — "education, not legal advice… qualified attorney… confirm with compliance office."
- *Rules currency:* B1 Ch. 1, Ch. 4; B2 Ch. 9 — "current as of July 2026 and change."
- *Banking/frameworks:* B1 Ch. 5 ("not specific accounts or institutions; Ducat is not a bank"), Ch. 6 (illustrative percentages).
- *Insurance:* B2 Ch. 11; B3 Ch. 9 (estate line added: "explains vocabulary, not documents").
- *Investing risk strip:* B3 Ch. 3, 4, 5, 6 — "Investing involves risk, including possible loss of principal. Past performance does not guarantee future results. Nothing in this chapter is a recommendation." (Ch. 5 strip extends to hypothetical-figures language.)
- *Professional-roles conflict strip:* B1 Ch. 12; B2 Ch. 7 — firm-is-an-adviser conflict named, "use these questions on everyone, including us."

**Layer 3 — Component-level labels.** Every narrative scene opens under a **"Fictional Example"** chip (see §4). Every worksheet carries the education-only footer (§2 table). All "What this means in real life" blocks open with an explicit *Hypothetical* label; Book 3's add "illustrative only / not a recommendation" where figures appear.

**Layer 4 — Back-matter full disclosures.** Master block restated verbatim + book-specific additions: B1 (rules-refresh note); B2 (contract-education and entity-education paragraphs); B3 (hypothetical-investment-examples block, risk sentences, Ch. 11 conflict cross-reference with CRD and lookup URL).

**Chapter 11 (Book 3) special disclosure.** The chapter opens with a bolded, boxed conflict-of-interest disclosure identifying Ducat as an adviser, directing readers to adviserinfo.sec.gov / CRD #331545 / Form ADV, and instructing them to apply the chapter's process to Ducat itself; the fee-model section names the firm's own model and references the ADV conflicts section. This exceeds typical practice deliberately and doubles as the series' most prominent Form ADV delivery cue.

## 4. Fictional/hypothetical example controls

- Every chapter-opening narrative in all 36 chapters carries the **Fictional Example** label; front-matter "How to Use This Book" sections define the label ("The situations are realistic; the people are not real. No story describes an actual client or an actual result.").
- Every quantitative walk-through is labeled **Hypothetical** at first word, with assumptions stated inline where math appears.
- No example implies a client relationship, an achieved result, or an endorsement. No real athletes, schools, brands, teams, or third-party marks are named in examples (fictional business names only: e.g., "Bayline Grill"). **CCO spot-check recommended:** scan for inadvertent real-entity collisions in fictional names before print.

## 5. Investment-content controls (Book 3 specific)

- **Compounding/fee illustrations** (Ch. 5 and its summary): $5,000 at an assumed flat illustrative 6%/40 yrs ≈ $51,000; 20 yrs ≈ $16,000; 1-point cost drag → ≈ $35,000; $13,400/40 yrs ≈ $138,000. Each instance states: assumed rate, "illustrative," "returns are never steady and may be worse or better," "not a prediction/promise." Verify arithmetic retained through design (numbers are static text).
- **No securities, funds, products, tickers, allocations, or providers named or described favorably.** Fund concept described generically; selection expressly routed to professionals.
- **Roth IRA discussion** is rule-description only (earned-income requirement; NIL generally qualifies; "limits apply — check current figures with your tax professional"). No contribution limits or rates are hard-coded anywhere in the series → nothing goes stale on annual IRS adjustments.
- **Fiduciary/fee-model chapter** describes fee-only, commission, and fee-based models neutrally, names the firm's own model with a conflicts pointer, and contains no comparative claim of superiority.
- **No individualized advice triggers:** all directive language is process-directive ("ask," "verify," "document"), never allocation-directive.

## 6. Launch-kit-specific notes

- All landing pages/emails carry the standard footer; investing-adjacent items (B3 pages/emails; captions flagged in kit) add the risk line. Captions without investment/outcome content omit the micro-line by design (space constraints; consistent with education-only content) — CCO may elect to append it universally as house style.

Consistent with the firm's prior decision to remove lead capture from the FRS calculator, ungated-first remains the recommended default.
- **Claims audit of marketing copy:** all superlatives are about the *books' scope*, not adviser services or outcomes; "protective/most valuable" phrasing reviewed and kept hedged ("can," "may," "helps you understand"). No performance implication located. The word "guaranteed" appears in marketing copy only inside scam-warning context (quoted as a red flag).
- **Social platform note:** these are first-party brand posts, not paid athlete endorsements; if the firm later pays any third party (including athletes) to promote the books, FTC endorsement disclosure and Marketing Rule endorsement analysis are triggered — separate approval required before any such arrangement.

## 7. Third-party references and IP hygiene

- Regulatory/venue references (NCAA, CSC/NIL Go, FHSAA, IRS, SEC, FINRA, FTC, adviserinfo.sec.gov, BrokerCheck) are nominative, factual, and non-affiliational; the non-affiliation disclosure covers them. No logos to be used in design (per Design Handoff imagery prohibitions).
