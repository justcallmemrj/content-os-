## 5. Voice & tone (Ducat is distinctive — match it)

Ducat's voice is **measured, discreet, disciplined, and premium.** It teaches calmly and never hypes. Mirror the site's register:

- **Do:** clear declarative sentences; words like *clarity, discipline, discretion, foundation, perspective, long-term, thoughtful, considerations.* Hedge honestly where reality is conditional ("may," "can," "depends on your situation"). Respect the reader's intelligence.
- **Don't:** hype, urgency, slang overload, hustle-culture energy, "get rich," "secure the bag," fear, or anything that reads as a sales pitch.
- **Balance for the audience:** the tone is elevated but never stiff or over-your-head for a college freshman. Warm enough for a parent, sharp enough to respect an 18-year-old earning real money for the first time. Define every term the first time it appears.

The feeling to leave the reader with: *calm, capable, and a little more in control of their money than they were 60 seconds ago.*

## 9. Caption system

Two versions per post. Feeds truncate, so **load the hook into the first ~125 characters** (Instagram) / **~140** (LinkedIn mobile) so the compelling line lands before "…more."

**This is a deep-dive, education-first campaign — captions must actually teach.** Every caption delivers at least one **concrete, verified, useful thing**: a real mechanism, a real rule/threshold (verified per Section 11), or a clear "here's how this works and why it matters." No platitudes, no vague encouragement. If a caption could have been written without any research, it isn't done.

**Instagram / Facebook** — accessible, clear, easy to skim. Short paragraphs, strong opening, plain language (define jargon on first use), one genuinely useful takeaway. **140–240 words.**

**LinkedIn** — same substance, slightly more depth and a professional register. **170–300 words.**

**Every caption includes, in order:**
1. Hook (the surprising or clarifying line)
2. The teach (the actual explanation / mechanism / number — this is the core, and it's the deepest part)
3. Why it matters for a student athlete or their family
4. **Question-CTA + save prompt** (below)
5. Compliance disclosure (Section 12)

**Question-CTA + save prompt (locked style — rotate; always a related question, always invite saving, never a pitch):**
- "Which of these did you already know? Save this to come back to before your next deal."
- "Have you and your family talked this one through yet? Save it for that conversation."
- "Do you know what your after-tax number really is? Save this for tax season."
- "What would you add to this list? Save it so it's here when you need it."
- "Not sure where you stand on this? Save this and revisit it when a decision is in front of you."
- "Who's on your financial team right now? Save this before you add anyone."

**Never** use acquisition CTAs ("book a call," "request a private conversation," "DM us," "work with us") or anything implying guaranteed results. The CTA's only job is reflection + saving.

## 11. Fact-check protocol (3 passes — verify, then double-check twice)

**Rule zero: never state a number, rate, threshold, or dated fact from memory. Pull it live at build time and log it.** If a claim can't be verified against a primary source, cut it or soften it to a non-numeric educational point. This space (NIL, collegiate compensation, athlete tax) moves quickly — treat every dollar figure and every rule as needing fresh confirmation.

### Primary / authoritative sources
- **NCAA policy:** ncaa.org — but what actually applies to a given athlete depends on their **school/conference compliance office and their state's law**, so point readers there.
- **State NIL law** and **state high-school athletic-association rules** for the athlete's state and level.
- **Taxes:** irs.gov (income, self-employment tax, estimated taxes, Roth IRA rules, reporting thresholds; Pubs 17, 505, 590-A).
- **Investing & vetting advisers:** SEC **Investor.gov** (basics, avoiding fraud); **FINRA BrokerCheck** (brokercheck.finra.org) and **SEC IAPD** (adviserinfo.sec.gov) for checking records.
- **Scams/fraud:** FTC (consumer.ftc.gov). **Credit/banking basics:** CFPB (consumerfinance.gov).
- Treat commercial NIL-news outlets as **secondary** — anchor every rule and number to a primary source and to the athlete's own school/state.

### Areas that change — re-verify on every build (do not assume)
- **Collegiate revenue sharing:** whether/how schools pay athletes directly, the annual per-school cap, and the current rules and start date (post–*House v. NCAA* settlement era) — verify current figures and status.
- **Third-party NIL deal review / clearinghouse** (e.g., a "NIL Go"–style process): thresholds and how it works — verify.
- **State NIL laws** — vary by state, change often; some permit direct school payments — verify by state.
- **High-school NIL rules** — vary by state association; some allow, some prohibit — verify by state and level.
- **NCAA / conference NIL policy** — evolving — verify.
- **Agent/representation rules** for NIL — vary by state and level — verify.
- **Tax figures:** self-employment tax rate, estimated-tax rules/dates, 1099-NEC/1099-K reporting thresholds, standard deduction, and multistate/residency rules — verify current-year at irs.gov and the relevant state.
- **Roth IRA:** current-year contribution limit and the earned-income rules that let a young earner contribute — verify at irs.gov.
- **Any dollar amount or date** — verify live.

### The 3 passes (every post must clear all three)
1. **Source accuracy** — Every factual/numeric claim has a primary-source citation in the claim register; nothing from memory; volatile items above are freshly verified. **Pass only if 100% cited from primary sources.**
2. **Educational balance & depth** — Accurate, not oversimplified; acknowledges that specifics depend on the athlete's state/school/level/situation; presents trade-offs fairly (no cherry-picking); and actually **teaches something concrete** (meets the depth bar in Section 9). **Pass if balanced, honest, and genuinely useful.**
3. **Compliance & clarity** — Screen against Section 12: disclosures present; no affiliation implied; no guarantees/performance promises; no individualized advice; no school marks or real-athlete likenesses; and the caption reads clearly at roughly an 8th–9th-grade level with the hook above the fold. **Pass if clean and clear; otherwise mark HOLD for my sign-off.**

**Then double-check:** after all three passes, re-run passes 1 and 3 one more time on the finalized text (the "double-check twice" step) — numbers and disclosures are where errors hide. Log the result.

### Claim register (one per post)

| Claim as written | Figure / fact | Source (name + URL) | Date accessed | Verbatim source language | Status (Pass / Adjusted) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

### Per-post fact-check log (compact)
`P1 ✅ / P2 ✅ / P3 ✅ — recheck P1 ✅ / P3 ✅ — Status: PASS` *(or ADJUSTED with note, or HOLD with reason)*. Nothing marked **HOLD** ships until I clear it.

## 12. Compliance framework

**Context:** Ducat Private Wealth is the public-facing brand of **Joy Financial Group LLC, a Florida-registered investment adviser** providing **fee-only** advisory services. Content must comply with applicable RIA advertising rules (Florida Rule 69W and the anti-fraud provisions modeled on SEC Rule 206(4)-1). *You produce a first-pass compliance screen; I am the firm's compliance officer and give final sign-off. Confirm all regulatory language matches the current Form ADV and the disclosures at ducatwealth.com/legal-information-disclosures before anything publishes.*

**Prohibited in any post:**
- Promising or implying results, guarantees, or specific outcomes; any performance claim.
- Recommending a specific investment, strategy, product, or action without context (that's individualized advice / a recommendation).
- Testimonials or endorsements without the disclosures the Marketing Rule requires.
- **Using any university, athletic-department, conference, league, team, or sponsor name, logo, mark, mascot, or color identity** in a way that implies affiliation or endorsement.
- **Using any real athlete's name, image, or likeness** without rights — keep posts general education, never "how [Athlete] made $X."
- Fear-based or hype framing; "get rich" energy.
- Presenting tax or legal guidance as individualized advice.
- Cherry-picked data or one-sided presentation of a decision that has trade-offs.

**Required disclosures (drawn from Ducat's own published disclosures; place in the caption — condense to what fits, keep the meaning intact):**

*Base (every post):*
> Educational and informational only — not individualized investment, financial, tax, legal, or accounting advice, and not a recommendation of any investment, strategy, or product. Ducat Private Wealth is the public-facing brand of Joy Financial Group LLC, a Florida-registered investment adviser (fee-only). Not affiliated with, sponsored by, or endorsed by any university, athletic department, league, or government agency.

*Add for NIL / eligibility posts:*
> NIL and collegiate-compensation rules vary by state, school, and level, and change frequently. Verify current rules with your school's compliance office and applicable state law before acting.

*Add for tax posts:*
> NIL and athlete income is generally taxable. Consult a qualified tax professional about your specific situation.

*Add for contracts posts:*
> Not legal advice. Have any agreement reviewed by a qualified attorney before you sign.

*Add for investing posts:*
> Investing involves risk, including possible loss of principal. Past performance is not indicative of future results, and no strategy can guarantee a profit or protect against loss.

Keep the frame clean — never legalistic. Full disclosures live in the caption and the master document.
