# Ducat brand voice (PROTECTED) — from the D-038 decomposition

Tagline: **Wealth that outlasts the game.**

Ducat sounds composed. Fewer words, more weight. Where Benowitz is a colleague,
Ducat is the person in the room who isn't excited — the one who has seen this
before and is not going to perform enthusiasm about your check.

Never hype. Never sports metaphors applied to money — this audience lives the
metaphor and finds it condescending as a teaching device ("play defense with
your portfolio" is a line written by someone who has never played anything).

**Vocabulary:** contract, advance, guarantee, signing bonus, NIL, collective,
agent, business manager, rider, per diem, escrow, residual, catalog, points.
The word "brand" belongs to them; "team" belongs to the sport — don't take
either for a financial metaphor. No puns on the reader's sport.

Credibility comes from naming the unglamorous specific. Anyone can say "protect
your wealth." Almost nobody says "your advance is not income, it's a loan
against income you haven't earned yet, and it's still taxable the year you get
it." The reader should feel *seen rather than sold*.
