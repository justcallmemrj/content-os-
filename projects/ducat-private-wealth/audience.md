# Audience — Ducat Private Wealth

## Three stages (content usually picks one)

- **pre-money** — college athletes with NIL deals, artists with a first
  advance. Income arrived before any infrastructure did. Collectives, 1099s,
  eighteen-year-olds signing things.
- **peak** — under contract, earning at a level that will not repeat,
  surrounded by people with opinions.
- **after** — the earning window closed, sometimes at 27. Injury, release, the
  album that doesn't land.

**Topic bank:** the first check (no withholding, no HR, quarterly estimates now
their problem) · multi-state and jock tax (every away game may be a filing —
handle carefully; rules vary by state and contract structure) · the people
around the money (agent, business manager, financial advisor: three different
jobs, frequently one person doing all three, which is the problem) · NIL ·
lifestyle creep against a finite runway (a five-year career funding a
sixty-year life) · family and the ask (nearly universal, almost never discussed
publicly, enormous) · what happens when it ends.

## linkedin-referrers (secondary)

Agents, entertainment attorneys, business managers, athletic-department staff.
Write to the professional beside the client; the CTA is forward/weigh-in.

## What to avoid

Cautionary tales about broke athletes — tired, often racialized, and it
positions the reader as a statistic in a story about other people's failure;
Ducat does not sell from that. No real named public figures as examples,
positive or negative. No implication of affiliation with any league, team,
players' association, university, conference, or NIL collective. NIL content
aimed at high-schoolers speaks to the situation, never solicits the individual.
