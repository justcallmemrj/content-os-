# st-positioning-offers (SK-B11)

## Positioning procedure

1. State the position as: for **[specific person]** facing **[specific
   moment]**, this brand is **[the specific answer]** — unlike **[the real
   alternative they'd otherwise choose]**. Every slot filled from project
   memory, not invented.
2. A position that could be pasted onto a competitor's site is not a
   position — rewrite until the unglamorous specific appears.
3. Positioning claims about the audience's situation are claims: anything
   factual routes through the ledger like all copy (D2/D3).

## Offer design

1. Offers are **access and education value** (a free webinar, a guide, a
   review session) stated plainly — never outcome promises ("save thousands",
   "maximize your payout"): the compliance envelope binds strategy documents
   exactly as it binds captions.
2. One offer per campaign; the exact string locks in the brief
   (SK-B10 rule 2) and rides every child verbatim.
3. The offer must be honorable as stated: free means free, live means live —
   publish riders are downstream consequences of offer words chosen here.

## Prohibited behavior

Outcome-promising offers; positioning by superlative ("best", "leading" —
lint-banned anyway); inventing audience facts; two offers in one campaign;
editing a locked offer mid-campaign (that's a new H1, not a tweak).
