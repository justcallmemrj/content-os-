# DROP exit LinkedIn — voice v2

Every spring, somewhere in Florida, a benefits coordinator gets the same knock. A teacher in her last year of DROP closes the door, lowers her voice, and asks: the pension part I understand, but what happens with the check?

Union reps get it in the parking lot. Sheriff's office HR gets it during exit paperwork. CPAs get it in March, sometimes a tax year too late. The first person an FRS member asks is almost never an advisor. It's you.

Here's the decision, stated plainly. The rule is hard: you must leave FRS-covered work for that balance to pay out. After that, your monthly pension turns on by itself. The lump sum doesn't. It waits for your instruction.

Take the check, and cash gets taxed as that year's ordinary income, stacked on everything else you earn. Any payout sent to you directly has twenty percent withheld for federal tax. A direct rollover is the other route. The money moves trustee to trustee into an IRA or another qualified plan, nothing withheld, still tax-deferred.

Choose cash, and it's taxed in the year it pays out. You can see that year coming. That's the difference between a deadline and runway.
