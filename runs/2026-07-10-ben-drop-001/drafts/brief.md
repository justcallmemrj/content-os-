# Creative brief — 2026-07-10-ben-drop-001

**Run:** 2026-07-10-ben-drop-001 · **Task:** T-2026-0710-004 · **Deliverable:** reel-90s script (instagram-reels) · **Objective:** webinar-registration · **Voice:** brand-voice.md@1.0.0 · **Source asset:** 01_DROP_Exit_Webinar_Script.md (AST-BEN-WEBINAR-SCRIPTS), Slides 7–8

- **Audience:** `audience.md#frs-50plus` — FRS members (teachers, deputies, firefighters, EMS, corrections, state/county staff) approaching or inside DROP. One pension, one DROP account, a small number of very large, mostly irreversible decisions. Suspicious of advisors — earned; respect it. Second person, their vocabulary (DROP, the Division of Retirement, years of service).

- **Problem:** At DROP exit, the accumulated balance becomes the largest single sum most members will ever hold at one time — and many don't know that *cash vs. direct rollover* changes how (and when) that money is taxed. The choice arrives at a hard, known termination date, and the Division of Retirement doesn't do financial planning, so nobody official is going to walk them through it.

- **Central lesson:** When your DROP money pays out, taking it as cash means it's taxed as that year's income, while a direct rollover keeps it tax-deferred — so how you take it is a real decision, not paperwork.

- **Hook direction:** Lead with the scale-plus-stakes of the moment — "the biggest check of your career comes with a decision attached" territory. Candidate mechanisms (deliver ≥3 hooks per SK-B3): (1) stakes/consequence — the same dollar amount, two very different tax outcomes; (2) known-date reframe — you can see this decision coming years away; (3) curiosity gap — "two arrows leave the DROP bucket; only one of them is automatic." No fear, no countdown urgency (urgency slider = 1); the termination date is a *known* date, which is the reassuring part — keep the webinar's "clarity, not perfection" posture.

- **Emotional movement:** vaguely aware → oriented and in control ("this is knowable, and I have time to understand it before my date").

- **CTA (single ask):** Register for the free DROP Exit webinar (spoken, one ask only — no "follow," no "book a call").

- **Length/format:** Instagram reel, ~90 seconds, **170–200 spoken words**, 7-beat reel structure. Disclosures go in a persistent on-screen block, never the spoken track.

- **Required facts:**
  - **F-BEN-0003** (frs.drop.termination-requirement) — REQUIRED. State the termination requirement as the hard rule it is; never soften to "you'll probably need to leave."
  - **F-BEN-0010** (frs.drop.lump-sum.tax-treatment) — REQUIRED. Cash = ordinary income in the distribution year, stacking on other income; direct rollover defers. Never quantify the tax cost for a stranger.
  - Supporting (available if needed): **F-BEN-0004** (exit mechanics — pension turns on, lump sum becomes payable), **F-BEN-0006** (mandatory 20% withholding on distributions paid to the participant), **F-BEN-0007** (direct rollover preserves deferral, no withholding).
  - Disclosures: **DISC-BEN-FRS-01** (per work order) **+ DISC-BEN-TAX-01** — this is tax content, so BEN-C5 adds the tax referral disclosure on top of the work order's list. On-screen block, verbatim, written out.

- **Prohibited territory:**
  - **No performance claims, projections, or guarantees** (envelope hard line 1): nothing of the form "save $X," "could cost you thousands," "maximize your payout," "most people leave money on the table." Frame tax treatment as a *consideration*, never a dollar *result*.
  - **No savings-dollar or quantified-outcome framings** — F-BEN-0010's usage note is explicit: never quantify the tax cost for a stranger.
  - **No testimonials, client stories, or "join hundreds of members"** (hard line 2).
  - **Education, not individualized advice** (hard line 3): how it works and what to ask — never "roll it over" as an instruction to the viewer.
  - **BEN-C1:** FRS non-affiliation stated (in the disclosure block). **BEN-C2:** no pension-solvency fear. **BEN-C3:** no implication FRS is hiding anything — "the Division of Retirement doesn't do financial planning" is the whole villain-free truth. **BEN-C4:** never suggest Benowitz can improve the pension itself; the decision space is *around* the pension. **BEN-C6:** "fee-only" on hold — use "fiduciary" if the firm is characterized at all.
  - No manufactured urgency (voice register, urgency=1); no real named public figures; no annuity-product bashing if Slide 8's IRA-location nuance is touched — compare, don't condemn.
