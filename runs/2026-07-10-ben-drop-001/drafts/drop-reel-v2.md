[TO CAMERA] Your DROP payout arrives as a single check. It comes with a decision attached. Most members never hear it called a decision.

[0:05 STAKES]
[TO CAMERA] Teachers, deputies, firefighters, state staff: if you're in DROP, it ends on a date you already know. The rule is hard: you must leave FRS-covered work for that balance to pay out.

[0:15 BEAT ONE]
[VO / B-ROLL: a single envelope landing on a kitchen table]
[TO CAMERA] Most members picture a payday. Half of that is right: your monthly pension turns on by itself. The lump sum doesn't. It waits for your instruction.

[0:35 BEAT TWO]
[TO CAMERA] Here's the part that rarely comes up: cash gets taxed as that year's ordinary income, stacked on everything else you earn. Any payout sent to you directly has twenty percent withheld for federal tax.

[0:55 BEAT THREE]
[TO CAMERA] A direct rollover is the other route. The money moves trustee to trustee into an IRA or another qualified plan, nothing withheld, still tax-deferred. The timing becomes yours.

[1:15 TURN]
[TO CAMERA] But that's not the real question. Choose cash, and it's taxed in the year it pays out. You can see that year coming. That's not a deadline. That's runway.
