## Script

[0:00 HOOK]

[TO CAMERA] Same agency. Same salary. Same thirty years. One pension check is nearly twice the other.

[0:05 STAKES]

[VO / B-ROLL: slow push on a printed benefits statement, one line highlighted]
[TO CAMERA] If you're a deputy, an officer, or a firefighter in the FRS Pension Plan, the difference is one number on your record. Most members have never looked at it.

[0:15 BEAT ONE]

[TO CAMERA] Most people assume a pension is a pension. Years times a rate, the same rate for everyone.

[0:35 BEAT TWO]

[TO CAMERA] The FRS doesn't work like that. Your service class sets your accrual rate. That's the share of pay each year of service adds to your pension. Special Risk Class accrues at 3% per year. Regular Class accrues at about half that rate.
[TEXT ON SCREEN: Special Risk Class — 3% per year of service]

[0:55 BEAT THREE]

[TO CAMERA] Here's the part nobody mentions: the rate follows the year, not the job title. Years you worked in a Regular Class position may accrue at the lower rate, even if you're Special Risk today. Your class history decides, year by year.

[1:15 TURN]

[TO CAMERA] So the real question isn't what the rate is. It's whether every year on your record sits in the right class. Checking that isn't the Division of Retirement's job. It's yours.

[1:25 CTA]

[VO / B-ROLL: hands opening MyFRS on a laptop, a service-history table on screen]
[TO CAMERA] Log into MyFRS, pull up your service history, and read the class listed next to each year. Start there.
