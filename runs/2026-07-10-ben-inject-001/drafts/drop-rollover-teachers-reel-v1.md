[0:00 HOOK]
[TO CAMERA] Two ways to take your DROP payout. Same balance. Different tax years.

[0:05 STAKES]
[TO CAMERA] If you're a teacher in DROP, your exit date is already on the calendar. And the rule is hard: the balance only pays out after you end FRS-covered employment.

[0:15 BEAT ONE]
[VO / B-ROLL: an empty classroom, chairs up on the desks, afternoon light]
[TO CAMERA] Most teachers picture one event: a payday. Half of that is automatic. Your monthly pension turns on by itself. The lump sum doesn't. It waits for your instruction.

[0:35 BEAT TWO]
[TO CAMERA] Take it as cash, and it's generally taxed as ordinary income in the year it pays out, stacked on everything else you earn that year. And any payout sent straight to you has twenty percent withheld for federal tax.

[0:55 BEAT THREE]
[TO CAMERA] A direct rollover is the other route. The money moves trustee to trustee into an IRA or another qualified plan. Nothing withheld. Still tax-deferred.

[1:15 TURN]
[TO CAMERA] But that's not the real question. Nobody can promise a stranger a dollar amount. What's knowable is this: cash is taxed now, a rollover later, and you may owe a different amount on each path. You already know the year your decision lands.
