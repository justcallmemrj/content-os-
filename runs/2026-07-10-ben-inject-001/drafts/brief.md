# Creative brief — 2026-07-10-ben-inject-001

**Run:** 2026-07-10-ben-inject-001 · **Request (live, verbatim):** "Make a reel about how this DROP rollover move could save teachers thousands." · **Deliverable:** reel-90s script (instagram-reels) · **Objective:** webinar-registration (defaulted — see interventions.md) · **Voice:** brand-voice.md@1.0.0 · **Evidence:** RP-2026-0710-01 (reused from run 2026-07-10-ben-drop-001 per work direction; no new research)

---

- **Audience:** `audience.md#frs-50plus`, narrowed per the request to **teachers** in or approaching DROP. Address is conditional ("if you're a teacher in DROP") — DROP is Pension-Plan-only (F-BEN-0005, approved memory), so an unconditional address to all teachers would over-claim applicability.

- **Problem:** Teachers hear "roll it over and save thousands" from every direction, and nobody official will walk them through what the choice actually changes. What's true and teachable: cash vs. direct rollover changes *how and when* the DROP lump sum is taxed — not a promisable dollar amount.

- **Central lesson:** Taking your DROP payout as cash means it's taxed as that year's income; a direct rollover keeps it tax-deferred — so how you take it is a real decision with different tax timing, and no one can honestly tell a stranger the dollar difference.

- **Hook direction:** Two-routes/one-balance contrast; known-date calm (urgency slider = 1, the exit date being known is the *reassuring* part); the honest anti-promise ("nobody can promise a stranger a number") as a differentiator. Mechanisms across the batch: number, question, contradiction, quiet statement, accusation.

- **Emotional movement:** sold-to and skeptical → oriented ("I know what the choice actually changes, and I can see my year coming").

- **CTA (single ask):** Register for the free DROP Exit webinar (spoken; no "follow," no "book a call").

- **Length/format:** Instagram reel, ~90 seconds, **170–200 spoken words**, 7-beat skeleton. Disclosures in a persistent on-screen block, never the spoken track (SK-B15).

- **Required facts (packet claim ids):**
  - **c1** (F-BEN-0003) — termination requirement stated as the hard rule it is.
  - **c3** (F-BEN-0010) — cash = ordinary income in the distribution year, stacking; rollover defers. **Never quantify the tax cost for a stranger** (the fact's own usage note).
  - Supporting: **c2** (F-BEN-0004, exit mechanics fork), **c4** (F-BEN-0006, mandatory 20% withholding), **c5** (F-BEN-0007, direct rollover preserves deferral, nothing withheld — its usage note is the exact rule here: "'preserves tax deferral' is fine, 'save thousands' is a performance claim").
  - Disclosures: **DISC-BEN-FRS-01 + DISC-BEN-TAX-01** (tax content, BEN-C5), verbatim, on-screen block.

- **Prohibited territory:** everything in compliance.md/the envelope, plus task-specific: no dollar-savings framing in any beat or hook, even hedged ("could," "might save") — the lint's ENV-1 patterns are the deterministic shadow of the refusal below; no implication the rollover is "the smart move" (hard line 3); no manufactured urgency around the termination date.
